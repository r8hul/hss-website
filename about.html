Our Story

About HSS Thailand

A cultural and service organisation rooted in Sanatana Dharma — nurturing character, leadership, and community spirit across Thailand.

Our Purpose

Why We Exist

Hindu Swayamsevak Sangh Thailand exists to serve the Hindu community and broader Thai society through the timeless principles of Dharma — righteousness, duty, and compassion. We believe that a strong community is built one individual at a time, through character, discipline, and service.

Our work is inspired by the tradition of swayamsevaks — volunteers who serve without expectation of reward. Every week, in shakhas across Bangkok and beyond, men, women, and children gather to build themselves and their community.

We are non-political, open to all Hindus regardless of background or tradition, and committed to fostering harmony both within the Hindu community and with the broader Thai society.

Our Mission

To cultivate confident individuals rooted in dharma who serve society selflessly, foster unity within the Hindu community, and work towards the well-being of all people in Thailand.

Our Vision

A vibrant, organised Hindu community in Thailand — one that contributes meaningfully to the nation while preserving its rich cultural and spiritual heritage for future generations.

Our Approach

Shakha-based learning that weaves together physical fitness, intellectual growth, cultural pride, and service. We build from the ground up — one individual, one family, one community at a time.

Our Journey

History of HSS Thailand

From humble beginnings to a thriving community — our journey in Thailand spans over fifteen years of dedicated service.

Early 2000s

Seeds Are Planted

A small group of dedicated Hindu families in Bangkok begins informal gatherings, inspired by the shakha tradition and a desire to preserve Hindu culture for their children growing up in Thailand.

2006

HSS Thailand Is Formally Established

With the blessings of HSS international, the Thailand chapter is formally constituted. The first official shakha begins in the On Nut area of Bangkok, laying the foundation for what would become a flourishing community.

2008–2012

Growth Across Bangkok

New shakhas open in Sukhumvit, Silom, and suburban Bangkok. Bala Gokulam for children launches, drawing families from across the city. Annual camps and festivals begin drawing larger and larger crowds.

2015

Seva Programmes Expand

HSS Thailand formalises its seva wing, partnering with local organisations to run food drives, educational support initiatives, and community clean-up projects across Bangkok.

2020–Present

Resilience & Renewal

Despite challenges posed by global disruptions, HSS Thailand adapted, moving some activities online and running intensive relief seva efforts. Today the organisation is stronger than ever, with six active shakhas and a growing membership.

"We did not come together simply as immigrants trying to preserve culture. We came together as swayamsevaks — volunteers committed to building something lasting for our community and our adopted home."
— HSS Thailand founding member

Global Family

HSS Worldwide

HSS Thailand is part of a global family of Hindu Swayamsevak Sangh chapters spanning over 50 countries and five continents.

🌍

Founded in 1966

HSS was founded in the United Kingdom in 1966 by swayamsevaks of the RSS who had settled abroad. Today it is the largest Hindu organisation outside India.

🌐

50+ Countries

From the UK and USA to Australia, Canada, Kenya, and across South-East Asia — HSS chapters nurture Hindu communities around the world with the same core values.

🏅

Thousands of Shakhas

Hundreds of shakhas operate globally every week, each one a small but powerful hub of character-building, cultural preservation, and community service.

HSS Thailand is proud to be part of this extraordinary global movement — a family bound not by geography, but by shared values and a shared vision.
