The Shakha Explained

What Is a Shakha?

The word shakha (शाखा) means "branch" in Sanskrit — a branch of the great tree of Hindu society. A shakha is a daily or weekly gathering of swayamsevaks that forms the foundational unit of HSS and the RSS tradition it is rooted in.

Every shakha follows a structured yet joyful programme that blends physical activity, intellectual engagement, cultural learning, and fellowship. It is a space where individuals grow, friendships deepen, and a sense of collective purpose is cultivated.

The shakha is open to all — children, youth, and adults. There is no subscription fee, no qualification required. You simply show up, and the shakha becomes part of your week.

🏃 Physical Activity

Surya namaskar, yogasanas, outdoor games, and exercises build physical fitness, coordination, and team spirit.

🎵 Geet & Prayer

Bhajans, shlokas, and the Sangh Prarthana connect participants with the spiritual dimension of dharmic life.

🧠 Bauddhik

Intellectual discussions on history, culture, dharma, and current affairs — cultivating informed, thinking individuals.

🤝 Fellowship

The bonds formed at shakha go beyond the hour — they become lifelong friendships grounded in shared values.

For Children

Bala Gokulam

Bala Gokulam (बाल गोकुलम्) — "the world of children" — is HSS's dedicated programme for younger swayamsevaks aged 5 to 15. It runs in parallel to adult shakhas at several of our Bangkok locations.

Sessions are fun, interactive, and deeply enriching. Children learn through stories from the Ramayana and Mahabharata, participate in arts and craft activities inspired by Hindu festivals, sing bhajans, and begin to learn simple Sanskrit shlokas and their meanings.

The programme is designed to give children growing up outside India a strong, joyful connection to their cultural identity — not through obligation, but through love and belonging.

Getting Started

How to Join a Shakha

Joining is simple. There are no forms, no fees, and no prerequisites. Just come along.

01

Contact Us

Reach out by email or Facebook to find the shakha closest to you and confirm timings.

02

Just Show Up

Arrive at the venue a few minutes early. Wear comfortable clothes suitable for outdoor activity.

03

Participate

Join in the activities, meet the other swayamsevaks, and experience the shakha for yourself.

04

Keep Coming Back

The real value of the shakha comes over time — through consistency, friendship, and growth.
